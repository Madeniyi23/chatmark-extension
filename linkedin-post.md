I bookmark 50+ browser tabs a week.

But I've never been able to bookmark the ONE paragraph inside an AI chat that actually changed how I think about a problem.

Here's the thing — if you use Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini regularly, you know this pain:

You're 45 minutes deep into a conversation. The AI gives you a response that's genuinely brilliant — a framework, a code pattern, a reframing of your entire approach. You think "I'll come back to this."

You never do. Because there's no way to.

You can bookmark the chat. You can't bookmark the moment.

Three weeks later you're scrolling through 12 conversations going "it was somewhere in that Claude thread... or was it ChatGPT?" Meanwhile you've had 40 more chats since then.

This kept happening to me. So I built something about it.

Introducing ChatMark — a Chrome extension that lets you highlight and bookmark specific sections within AI conversations. Not the whole chat. The exact paragraph. The exact insight.

Here's what it does:

Select any text in Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini. A floating button appears. One click saves that section with tags, notes, and a deep link back to the exact spot in the conversation.

Want to find it later? Search across all your bookmarks. Filter by platform. Filter by tag. Click "Go to chat" and it scrolls you right back to that paragraph — highlighted in purple so you can't miss it.

It uses Chrome's Text Fragments API under the hood (the same tech behind Google's "link to highlight" feature) combined with a JavaScript fallback, so the scroll-to-text actually works reliably even on platforms that auto-scroll to the bottom when you open a conversation.

What surprised me most while building this: nothing like it exists. There are tab managers, session savers, full-page bookmarkers — but nothing that lets you pin a specific section of an AI chat and jump back to it weeks later.

I think this points to something bigger. We're generating more knowledge through AI conversations than ever before, but we have almost zero infrastructure for organizing that knowledge. Your AI chats are becoming a second brain — but it's a brain with no index, no retrieval system, and no way to cross-reference insights across platforms.

The tools will catch up. But right now, there's a gap.

ChatMark is open source and free. I just pushed it to GitHub and I'd love early feedback before submitting to the Chrome Web Store.

If you've ever lost a golden response in a sea of AI chats, give it a try. And if you have ideas for making it better, I'm all ears.
